Crispy Samosa

⭐ 4.9🕐 30 min🍽️ 4 servings

The ultimate Indian snack — crispy, golden, and filled with spiced potato and peas. Perfect with mint chutney.

Prep Time15 min
Cook Time15 min
DifficultyMedium
Spice Level🌶️ Medium

📝 Ingredients

2 cups flour · 3 large potatoes · 1 cup peas · 1 tsp cumin seeds · 1 tsp garam masala · 1 tsp coriander powder · Salt to taste · Oil for frying

👨‍🍳 Steps

  1. Mix flour with salt and oil, add water slowly to form a stiff dough. Rest for 20 min.
  2. Boil and mash potatoes. Mix in cooked peas, cumin, garam masala, coriander powder, and salt.
  3. Roll dough into thin circles, cut in half, form cones, fill with potato mixture, and seal edges with water.
  4. Deep fry on medium heat until golden and crispy (about 12–15 min). Serve hot with mint chutney.

Chicken Biryani

⭐ 5.0🕐 60 min🍽️ 6 servings

The king of Indian dishes. Fragrant basmati rice layered with tender chicken, fried onions, and saffron milk.

Prep Time20 min
Cook Time45 min
DifficultyHard
Spice Level🌶️🌶️ Hot

📝 Ingredients

500g basmati rice · 800g chicken · 2 onions (sliced) · 1 cup yogurt · 2 tbsp biryani masala · Saffron milk · Ghee · Mint leaves · Salt

👨‍🍳 Steps

  1. Marinate chicken with yogurt, biryani masala, and salt for at least 2 hours.
  2. Fry onions until golden brown. Set aside half for garnish.
  3. Cook marinated chicken in a heavy pot until 70% done. Layer par-boiled rice on top.
  4. Add saffron milk, ghee, fried onions, and mint. Seal with dough or foil and cook on dum (low heat) for 25 min.

Gulab Jamun

⭐ 4.9🕐 40 min🍽️ 20 pieces

Heavenly soft milk-solid balls soaked in fragrant rose and cardamom sugar syrup. India's favourite sweet.

Prep Time10 min
Cook Time30 min
DifficultyEasy
Spice LevelNone

📝 Ingredients

1 cup milk powder · ¼ cup flour · ¼ tsp baking soda · Ghee or butter · Milk to knead · Oil for frying · 2 cups sugar · Rose water · Cardamom

👨‍🍳 Steps

  1. Mix milk powder, flour, and baking soda. Rub in 2 tsp ghee. Add milk slowly to form a soft dough.
  2. Make a sugar syrup with 2 cups sugar, 1.5 cups water, rose water, and cardamom. Keep warm.
  3. Roll dough into smooth balls (no cracks). Deep fry on very low heat, stirring continuously until dark golden.
  4. Drop hot jamuns into warm syrup. Soak for at least 2 hours before serving.

Masala Chai

⭐ 4.8🕐 10 min🍽️ 2 cups

The soul of Indian mornings. A warming blend of tea, milk, ginger, and aromatic spices in every sip.

Prep Time2 min
Cook Time8 min
DifficultyEasy
Spice LevelMild warm

📝 Ingredients

1 cup water · 1 cup milk · 2 tsp black tea leaves · 1-inch ginger (grated) · 3 cardamom pods (crushed) · 1 cinnamon stick · 2 cloves · Sugar to taste

👨‍🍳 Steps

  1. Bring water to boil. Add ginger, cardamom, cinnamon, and cloves. Simmer 3 min.
  2. Add tea leaves and simmer for another 2 min until strong and fragrant.
  3. Pour in milk and bring to a rolling boil. Add sugar to taste.
  4. Strain into cups and serve hot with samosas or biscuits.

Masala Dosa

⭐ 4.7🕐 25 min🍽️ 4 servings

South India's crispy thin crepe made from fermented rice-lentil batter, filled with spiced potato masala.

Prep TimeOvernight soak
Cook Time25 min
DifficultyMedium
Spice Level🌶️ Mild

📝 Ingredients

2 cups rice · ½ cup urad dal · Salt · Oil · 4 boiled potatoes · Onion · Mustard seeds · Turmeric · Curry leaves · Green chillies

👨‍🍳 Steps

  1. Soak rice and dal separately for 8 hours. Grind together into smooth batter. Ferment overnight.
  2. For masala: sauté onions, mustard seeds, curry leaves, green chillies. Add mashed potatoes and turmeric.
  3. Heat a tawa (griddle). Pour a ladle of batter, spread in circles to make a thin crepe.
  4. Drizzle oil on edges. Once crisp, place masala filling in center, fold, and serve with sambar and chutney.

Vada Pav

⭐ 4.9🕐 25 min🍽️ 4 servings

Mumbai's beloved street food — a spiced potato fritter sandwiched in soft bread rolls with fiery chutneys.

Prep Time10 min
Cook Time15 min
DifficultyEasy
Spice Level🌶️🌶️ Spicy

📝 Ingredients

4 large potatoes (boiled) · 1 cup chickpea flour · Mustard seeds · Curry leaves · Green chillies · Turmeric · 4 pav buns · Dry garlic chutney · Oil

👨‍🍳 Steps

  1. Mash potatoes. Season with fried mustard seeds, curry leaves, green chillies, and turmeric. Form into balls.
  2. Make batter with chickpea flour, salt, turmeric, and water. Coat potato balls in batter.
  3. Deep fry vadas until crispy and golden on all sides.
  4. Slit pav buns, spread garlic chutney and green chutney. Place hot vada inside and serve immediately.
