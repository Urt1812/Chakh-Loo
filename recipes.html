Aloo Samosa

⭐ 4.9🕐 40 min🍽️ 6 pcs

The iconic crispy samosa with spiced aloo filling. Serve with Kaale Chole, Matar Chole, or Aloo Chutney.

Prep20 min
Cook20 min
Serves6
Type🌿 Pure Veg

Steps:

  1. Prepare the dough with maida, salt, and water. Rest for 20 minutes.
  2. Boil and mash potatoes. Mix with cumin, coriander, green chilli, and garam masala.
  3. Roll out dough into thin circles, cut in half and form cones.
  4. Fill each cone with potato mixture and seal the edges firmly.
  5. Deep fry in Pure Desi Ghee on medium heat until golden and crispy.
  6. Serve hot with your choice of chole or chutney.

Paneer Samosa

⭐ 4.8🕐 35 min🍽️ 6 pcs

Crispy samosa shells filled with richly spiced fresh paneer — a premium vegetarian favourite.

Prep15 min
Cook20 min
Serves6
Type🌿 Pure Veg

Steps:

  1. Prepare samosa dough with maida and rest for 20 minutes.
  2. Crumble paneer and sauté with onion, green chilli, garam masala, and coriander leaves.
  3. Roll dough, form cones, and fill with paneer mixture.
  4. Seal edges firmly and deep fry in Pure Desi Ghee until golden.
  5. Serve hot with mint chutney.

Litti Chokha (Aata, Baked)

⭐ 5.0🕐 60 min🍽️ 4 servings

Bihar's pride — whole wheat aata balls stuffed with sattu, baked on fire, dipped in pure Desi Ghee, served with smoky chokha.

Prep30 min
Cook30 min
Serves4
Type🌿 Pure Veg

Steps:

  1. Knead whole wheat aata dough until smooth and pliable.
  2. Prepare sattu filling: mix sattu with ajwain, onion, green chilli, salt, and lemon juice.
  3. Stuff each dough ball with sattu mixture and seal tightly.
  4. Bake on open charcoal fire until charred and fully cooked through.
  5. Dip hot littis generously in Pure Desi Ghee.
  6. For chokha: roast and mash brinjal and tomatoes with mustard oil, green chilli, and salt.
  7. Serve with chokha, green chutney, and sliced onions.

Litti Chokha (Maida, Fried)

⭐ 4.9🕐 45 min🍽️ 4 servings

Maida litti stuffed with sattu filling, deep fried in Desi Ghee until golden crispy, served with smoky chokha.

Prep20 min
Cook25 min
Serves4
Type🌿 Pure Veg

Steps:

  1. Knead maida dough with salt and a little Desi Ghee. Rest 15 minutes.
  2. Prepare sattu filling: mix sattu with ajwain, onion, green chilli, salt, and lemon juice.
  3. Stuff each dough ball with sattu mixture and seal tightly.
  4. Deep fry in Pure Desi Ghee on medium heat until golden and crispy all around.
  5. For chokha: roast and mash brinjal and tomatoes with mustard oil, chilli, and salt.
  6. Serve hot litti with chokha, chutney, and onion.

Gur Thekua

⭐ 4.9🕐 45 min🍽️ 20 pcs

Traditional Mithila Chhath Puja sweet — whole wheat flour and jaggery shaped and fried in pure Desi Ghee.

Prep20 min
Cook25 min
Makes~20 pcs
Type🌿 Pure Veg

Steps:

  1. Melt jaggery (gur) in warm water to make a syrup. Strain if needed.
  2. Mix whole wheat flour with fennel seeds and cardamom powder.
  3. Gradually add gur syrup and knead into a firm dough — do not add water.
  4. Shape using a traditional thekua mould or press flat with a fork.
  5. Deep fry in Pure Desi Ghee on low-medium heat until deep golden.
  6. Cool completely before storing — they stay fresh for several days.

Maida Nimki

⭐ 4.7🕐 30 min🍽️ 30 pcs

Crispy, flaky, perfectly salted Bihari namkeen made from maida — best with evening chai.

Prep15 min
Cook15 min
Makes~30 pcs
Type🌿 Pure Veg

Steps:

  1. Mix maida with ajwain, black pepper, salt, and a pinch of kalonji.
  2. Rub in melted Desi Ghee (moyan) until the flour is crumbly.
  3. Add cold water gradually and knead into a stiff dough. Rest 10 minutes.
  4. Roll out thin and cut into diamond or square shapes.
  5. Deep fry in Pure Desi Ghee on medium-low heat until crispy and golden.
  6. Cool fully and store in an airtight container.
